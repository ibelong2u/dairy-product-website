Nutritional Information about Goat Milk

Although Americans are accustomed to cows' milk, goats' milk is more common in most other parts of the world. Goats' milk is more beneficial to health than cows' milk; it is a complete protein and contains every essential amino acid.

Benefits of goat's milk:

  • great source of calcium (1 cup contains 33% of daily intake) and vitamin B-2 (20%  of daily intake)
  • contains more calcium, protein, vitamin A, riboflavin and beneficial minerals per serving than cow's milk
  • easy to digest because goats' milk has smaller fat globules
  • naturally homogenized 
  • helps to prevent anemia and bone demineralization
  • helps with digestive and metabolic diseases by utilizing minerals
  • contains less of bad enzymes than cows' milk

What are the magic components of goat's milk?

Goat's milk aids digestion because it has more medium chain triglycerides than cows' milk. It also contains less of the enzyme xanthine oxidase than cow's milk, which helps to maintain a healthy heart.
