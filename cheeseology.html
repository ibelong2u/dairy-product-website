Cheeseology

Making farmstead cheese truly is a science. Milk properties and composition change with the seasons as does the taste. In spring when the babies are young, the milk is more thin.  In fall, milk becomes more viscous and creamy.  So -- recipes for cheese need to be tweaked as the character of the milk changes.  

Our cheese is unique to anywhere in the world because it is flavored by the vegetation eaten by the goats in the Loess Hills.   Goats' milk can be used to create the same types of cheese that cows' milk does.  What distinguishes the variety of cheese made is the type of culture and how the cheese is physically manipulated.   Is it cut, put in a salt brine, aged?

Our chevre takes four days from mama to cheese.  Fresh stuff!   We make cheese every two to three days and each day we are working on some stage of cheese making.  Feta will be available in 2013 and it is aged in a salt brine for 6 weeks.
